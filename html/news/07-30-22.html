WVSU Student Regent conducts consultation on external campuses

by Arvin Vanne Ryan Alayon
July 30, 2022

WVSU UPDATE - Student Regent, Hershey Geline Sumpay visits 5 external WVSU Campuses to hold student consultations and discuss internationalization engagement of WVSU on June 28, 2022.

KaaThe Student Regent, also identified as the Federated Student Council Chairperson visited WVSU Calinog Campus, College of Agriculture and Forestry, WVSU Lambunao Campus, WVSU Janiuay, and going to WVSU Pototan.

Sumpay heard the student's concerns from existing campuses inside panay and discussed the plans of Student Ambassadors for the University's Internationalization.

"Our term in the FSC is going to end soon, and congratulations guid sa aton, kag sa inyo kay as pioneer student leaders nga nakapungko amidst the pandemic, na push through ta aton mga events."

Sumpay added in her remarks during the discussion with Agriculture and Forestry Student Council.

Sumpay collates all their concerns, and raised them to the Board of Regents, as part of her student report. This were the concerns that the campuses have raised:

1. The lack of operational guidance counselors.

Guidance counselors does a great job in helping the students cope with stressors that could naturally occur in the academe. Lack thereof could affect the wellbeing of our academic community.

2. The slow production of Identification Cards.

As a new system of printing and issuance of ID focuses in utilizing one publishing house, the problem of the slow issuance became a hindrance to several campuses, as their students will be graduating but renewal of their student I.D. is still on going.

3. An accessible online learning platform.

The students reported that in their campuses, they have been using several learning platforms in different subjects. They raised that they were using the Messenger app for some subjects, Google classroom for some, and other mediums were also considered. They raised that these could be overwhelming for the students as opening many application seems a great challenge in management of their study habits.

Hearing these concerns, the Student Regent assured that these concerns will be brought to the Board of Regents in their next meeting.

Sumpay came to visit these campuses with the FSC Vice Chairperson, Louela Marie Prado, FSC Public Information Officer Arvin Vanne Ryan Alayon, and USC Councilors Ronald Kim Pinuela and Hannah Binarao.
